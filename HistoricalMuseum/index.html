About the Museum

The Schleisingerville to Slinger Historical Museum was founded on November 14, 2019 to celebrate the 150th anniversary of the founding of Slinger. It is named after the late Edward H. Wolf who, with his wife Minette in 1984 purchased the deteriorating depot from the Village of Slinger and moved the building to its current location. Ed and his family remodeled the depot to become the headquarters for E.H. Wolf & Sons. They are a passionate and giving family to the Village of Slinger and its residents. Without their vision, determination and hard work this museum would not be possible. It is now a solid creation, in spirit and reality, and will be located at the Union Depot that Ed and family restored. This is the only active public Historical Museum in Slinger and involves dozens of Slinger residents, past and present, and friends in its collections, volunteering, sustenance, and management. All funds or artifacts received by other groups purporting to be a Slinger Museum of any sort have never donated, given, or transferred any artifacts, money, or material to the Edward H. Wolf Schleisingerville to Slinger Historical Museum.
